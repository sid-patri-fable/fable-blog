## Navattic

<img alt="Navattic" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/bbc8da7a-b52d-4f4d-b9b0-c432c8c70bcd"></img>

Navattic is one of the pioneers in the interactive demo space, known for its product tour software, especially popular with mid-sized companies. While it excels at fostering collaboration and supporting teamwork, the absence of a free trial, slow setup process, and a starting price of $500 per month may deter some potential users.

### Navattic Pros

- **Customizable templates:** Navattic offers a variety of templates that allow for quick customizations, making them adaptable to different use cases.

- **Scalability:** Navattic empowers companies to scale their demo creation, helping them engage multiple prospects simultaneously—a great advantage for fast-growing startups.

- **Integration capabilities:** It integrates seamlessly with other tools like HubSpot and Salesforce and provides analytics to help optimize demo performance.

### Navattic Cons

- **Higher pricing:** Navattic’s pricing might be too high for smaller businesses with tighter budgets.

- **No demo hub/demo center:** As demo hubs become crucial for marketing strategies, Navattic’s lack of a centralized demo center can be limiting.

- **Limited mobile experience:** Some users have noted that Navattic’s mobile responsiveness falls short, with demos not functioning as smoothly on mobile as on desktop.

### Exploring alternatives to Navattic

Due to the lack of a free trial, slower setup, and high pricing, some users may want to explore [alternative products](https://www.sharefable.com/blog/navattic-alternatives-and-competitors-in-2024?ref=navattic-vs-testbox-vs-fable-best-demo-platform). Starting at $500/month on annual billing, Navattic’s offering can be difficult to commit to without a chance to test it first.

## Testbox

<img alt="Testbox" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/bce5d344-0faa-40d8-afb2-3cadf1894d9b"></img>

Testbox enables sales teams to build custom sandbox environments, preloaded with relevant data, tailored for each prospect. It's particularly useful for teams seeking data-rich proof-of-concept (POC) demonstrations to showcase their product’s capabilities.

### Testbox Pros

- **Up-to-date demos:** As Testbox operates as a live instance, your demo remains updated with every product release.

- **User-level analytics:** It provides advanced insights into prospect behavior with detailed analytics.

- **Pre-configured data sets:** AEs can select custom data sets that are tailored for specific industries, enhancing the relevance of demos.

### Testbox Cons

- **Significant engineering effort:** Testbox requires substantial input from your engineering team since it runs as a live instance of your product.

- **Limited product range:** The number of software products supported by Testbox is limited, which could be restrictive for teams needing a broader range.

- **Limited integrations:** Testbox offers fewer integrations with other tools, which may limit its functionality for teams working with complex third-party workflows.

### Exploring alternatives to Testbox

Due to the need for engineering input and a hefty price tag of $38,000/year, Testbox may be a costly option for some businesses, especially with various [Testbox alternatives](https://www.sharefable.com/blog/testbox-alternatives-and-competitors-in-2024?ref=navattic-vs-testbox-vs-fable-best-demo-platform) available on the market.

## Fable

<FableEmbed src="https://app.sharefable.com/embed/demo/home-page-demo-desktop-v5yq1iykpaila806" />

Fable is built for marketing and sales teams of any size, offering highly customizable interactive demos to enhance engagement and simplify the sales process. With advanced analytics and seamless integrations, Fable stands as a strong tool for improving sales performance.

### Fable Pros

- **AI demo co-pilot:** Fable allows you to create demos in less than 5 minutes, generate relevant demo content, switch between different languages, create brand-specific themes, and automatically generate modules using simple prompts.

- **Demo hubs:** Fable offers advanced demo hubs, allowing teams to organize and customize multiple demos, and visitors can personalize their journey through advanced branching.

- **Mobile responsive demos:** Fable ensures demos are fully responsive across mobile devices, providing a smooth experience for users.

- **Deep customizations:** The platform offers on-screen HTML customization, allowing users to modify, blur, hide, or remove elements, giving teams immense control over demo customization.

### Fable Cons

- **Limited user seats:** Fable restricts the number of available user seats, which may not suit larger teams.

- **Product replication:** Fable replicates your product for demo purposes, which limits direct editing or customization of the live product.

## Feature comparison

### Free trial

- **Fable:** Offers a 14-day free trial for all plans and includes a free plan to create one demo.

- **Navattic:** No free trial, but offers a free tier similar to Fable for creating your first demo.

- **Testbox:** Does not offer a free trial.

### AI demos

- **Fable:** With Quilly, Fable’s AI demo co-pilot, you can generate demo content in any language, create themes based on your brand, auto-create modules, and more with simple prompts.

- **Navattic:** Currently does not support AI-powered demos.

- **Testbox:** Does not yet offer AI-powered demos.

### Responsive design

- **Fable:** Fable ensures a seamless experience across all devices by optimizing demos for mobile use.

- **Navattic:** Does not provide support for creating mobile-optimized demos.

- **Testbox:** As Testbox operates on your live product, its mobile responsiveness depends entirely on how your product is designed.

### Demo hubs

- **Fable:** Provides advanced demo hubs that allow the creation of demo collections, enabling personalized demo journeys.

- **Navattic:** Does not currently support demo hubs or centralized demo centers.

- **Testbox:** Testbox does not offer demo hubs or demo centers.
