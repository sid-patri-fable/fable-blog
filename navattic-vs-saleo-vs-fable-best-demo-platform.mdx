## Navattic

<img alt="Navattic" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/bbc8da7a-b52d-4f4d-b9b0-c432c8c70bcd"></img>

Navattic is a pioneer in the interactive demo space, popular among mid-sized companies for its product tour software. While it excels at promoting collaboration and supporting teamwork, its lack of a free trial, slower setup process, and starting price of $500 per month may deter some users.

### Navattic pros

- **Customizable templates:** Navattic offers a range of templates, allowing for fast customization to suit various use cases.

- **Scalability:** It empowers companies to scale demo creation, enabling them to engage multiple prospects at once—ideal for fast-growing startups.

- **Seamless integrations:** Navattic integrates with popular tools like HubSpot and Salesforce, offering analytics to optimize demo performance.

### Navattic cons

- **Higher pricing:** Navattic’s pricing may be too high for smaller businesses with limited budgets.

- **No demo hub:** The lack of a centralized demo hub can be a drawback for teams looking to manage multiple demos.

- **Mobile limitations:** Some users have reported that Navattic’s mobile responsiveness isn’t as smooth as its desktop experience.

### Exploring alternatives to Navattic

Given the lack of a free trial, high pricing, and slower setup, some teams might prefer exploring [alternative options](https://www.sharefable.com/blog/navattic-alternatives-and-competitors-in-2024?ref=navattic-vs-saleo-vs-fable-best-demo-platform). At $500 per month, Navattic’s pricing may be a barrier without a chance to test it first.

## Saleo

<img alt="Saleo" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/40898ba2-9960-43c5-bddd-768542540731"></img>

Saleo is built specifically for sales teams, allowing them to tailor demos by overlaying custom data onto product interfaces. This personalization helps create more relevant demos for prospects, streamlining the demo preparation process.

### Saleo pros

- **Custom data overlays:** Saleo enables teams to overlay personalized data onto product interfaces, making demos more relevant to the prospect’s needs.

- **Enhanced engagement:** By personalizing demo content, Saleo increases engagement and clarity for prospects.

- **Low maintenance:** Once set up, Saleo’s demo environments require minimal upkeep, ensuring your demos remain current with little ongoing effort.

### Saleo cons

- **Sales-focused:** Saleo’s primary focus on sales leaves it lacking features that may benefit marketing or customer success teams.

- **Dependence on support:** Updating demo data often requires help from Saleo’s support team, which may delay updates.

- **Complex initial setup:** The setup process can be complicated, especially for teams with intricate demo environments, often requiring collaboration with Saleo’s support team.

### Exploring alternatives to Saleo

Given the complex setup and reliance on support for updates, teams might look into [alternative platforms](https://www.sharefable.com/blog/saleo-alternatives-and-competitors-in-2024?ref=navattic-vs-saleo-vs-fable-best-demo-platform), particularly since Saleo’s starting price of $40,000 annually may be prohibitive for smaller teams.

## Fable

Fable is a flexible platform built for marketing and sales teams of all sizes, providing customizable interactive demos that enhance engagement and simplify the sales process. With advanced analytics and seamless integrations, Fable helps teams boost sales efficiency.

### Fable pros

- **AI-driven demo creation:** Fable’s Quilly AI co-pilot allows users to create demos in under five minutes, generate relevant content, switch between languages, and create brand-specific themes with just a few prompts.

- **Demo hubs:** Fable offers advanced demo hubs, enabling teams to manage and personalize multiple demos, and providing visitors with personalized journeys through branching options.

- **No engineering needed:** Fable is designed for ease of use, allowing marketing and sales teams to create and manage demos without requiring engineering support.

- **Mobile-optimized demos:** Fable ensures that all demos are fully responsive on mobile devices, providing a seamless experience for users.

### Fable cons

- **Limited user seats:** Fable restricts the number of available user seats, which may not be ideal for larger teams.

- **Demo replication limitations:** Fable replicates your product for demo purposes, limiting direct edits or customization of the live product during demos.

## Feature comparison

### Free trial

- **Fable:** Provides a 14-day free trial for all plans and includes a free tier to create one demo.

- **Navattic:** No free trial, though a free tier similar to Fable is available for creating your first demo.

- **Saleo:** Does not offer a free trial option.

### AI demos

- **Fable:** With Quilly, Fable’s AI-powered co-pilot, users can create demos in multiple languages, generate themes, and automatically create modules with simple prompts.

- **Navattic:** Does not currently support AI-driven demos.

- **Saleo:** Does not offer AI-powered demos.

### Setup time

- **Fable:** Offers zero setup time, enabling users to sign up and start creating demos immediately.

- **Navattic:** Requires setup by Navattic’s team before you can access the platform.

- **Saleo:** Typically takes several weeks to set up, requiring close collaboration between the team and Saleo’s support.

### Demo hubs

- **Fable:** Provides advanced demo hubs that enable the creation of demo collections and personalized demo journeys.

- **Navattic:** Does not currently support demo hubs.

- **Saleo:** Lacks demo hubs or demo centers.
