## Storylane

<img alt="Storylane" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/9b109d1f-c593-4b69-add6-f9baacabd2c5"></img>

Storylane makes it simple to create interactive product demos, helping teams set up and deploy custom demos quickly. With its easy-to-use interface and affordable pricing, it’s a top choice for growing SaaS businesses.

### Storylane pros

- **Easy to use:** Storylane’s intuitive interface allows users to build demos without needing technical expertise.

- **Responsive customer support:** Storylane is praised for its customer service, offering fast and reliable help through various channels.

- **Flexible demo creation:** The platform supports both screenshot-based and HTML capture demos, offering versatility for different use cases.

### Storylane cons

- **License requirement:** To use HTML capture demos, Storylane requires a minimum of five user licenses, which may be more than some teams need.

- **Limited device targeting:** Storylane doesn’t offer device-specific segmentation, restricting more tailored viewer experiences.

- **User seat limitations:** Larger teams may find the limited number of user seats restrictive, potentially hampering wider access across departments.

### Exploring alternatives to Storylane

While Storylane is great for teams needing quick and simple demo creation, its limited customization and basic analytics may prompt users to explore [other platforms](https://www.sharefable.com/blog/storylane-alternatives-and-competitors-in-2024?ref=storylane-vs-walnut-vs-fable-best-demo-platform) that offer more advanced features.

## Walnut

<img alt="Walnut" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/b97dbf32-27ab-428e-922d-dba6b08d5af4"></img>

Walnut is built with sales teams in mind, offering a platform for creating interactive, no-code demos for live presentations or follow-up engagements. With its easy customization options, it helps streamline the sales process by making presentations more engaging.

### Walnut pros

- **Collaborative demos:** Walnut enables prospects to comment and collaborate directly within the demo, boosting engagement and interaction.

- **Personalization options:** The platform offers robust features for sales teams to create highly tailored demos for individual prospects.

- **Reusable templates:** Walnut provides demo templates that sales reps can reuse and quickly customize for different prospects, saving time and effort.

### Walnut cons

- **No free trial:** Walnut doesn’t offer a free trial, which can make it difficult for teams to test the platform before committing.

- **Complexity for simple demos:** Teams that only need basic demos might find Walnut’s feature set too complicated.

- **Steep learning curve:** Walnut’s extensive features can lead to a steeper learning curve, making it harder for beginners to get started.

### Exploring alternatives to Walnut

Due to the absence of a free trial, complex features, and a starting price of $10,000 per year, teams might explore [alternative platforms](https://www.sharefable.com/blog/walnut-alternatives-and-competitors-in-2024?ref=storylane-vs-walnut-vs-fable-best-demo-platform) that offer a simpler setup and more flexibility.

## Fable

<FableEmbed src="https://app.sharefable.com/embed/demo/home-page-demo-desktop-v5yq1iykpaila806" />

Fable is a highly customizable platform that caters to marketing and sales teams looking to create interactive demos. Its advanced analytics, AI-powered features, and seamless integrations make it a go-to solution for engaging prospects and simplifying the sales process.

### Fable pros

- **AI-driven demo creation:** Fable’s Quilly AI co-pilot assists users in creating demo content, themes, and modules quickly and easily with just a few prompts.

- **Demo hubs:** Fable offers advanced demo hub functionality, allowing users to manage and customize multiple demos, providing prospects with personalized experiences.

- **Wide integration support:** Fable integrates with popular tools like HubSpot, Salesforce, Mailchimp, and Pipedrive, making it easy to manage and track demo data.

- **Advanced customization:** Fable allows teams to edit, hide, blur, or remove on-screen HTML elements, offering deep customization for diverse go-to-market needs.

### Fable cons

- **Limited user seats:** Fable places restrictions on the number of available user seats, which may limit larger teams’ access across departments.

- **Demo replication limitations:** Fable creates a replica of your product for demo purposes, meaning you cannot make live edits to the product during the demo.

## Feature comparison

### Free trial

- **Fable:** Provides a 14-day free trial on all plans and offers a free option to create a single demo.

- **Storylane:** Offers a free trial for screenshot-based demos, but not for HTML capture demos.

- **Walnut:** Does not offer a free trial.

### AI demos

- **Fable:** Quilly, Fable’s AI-powered co-pilot, helps users generate demo content, themes, and modules in multiple languages.

- **Storylane:** Features AI-powered tools for simplifying voiceover creation in demos.

- **Walnut:** Supports AI-powered demos that assist sales teams in demo creation.

### Responsive design

- **Fable:** Fully optimized for mobile and desktop to ensure seamless experiences on any device.

- **Storylane:** Does not support responsive demo creation.

- **Walnut:** Offers responsive design features to help create demos suitable for various devices.

### Demo hubs

- **Fable:** Features advanced demo hub capabilities, enabling teams to manage multiple demos and personalize user experiences.

- **Storylane:** Provides a basic demo hub for managing multiple demos.

- **Walnut:** Does not offer demo hub functionality.
