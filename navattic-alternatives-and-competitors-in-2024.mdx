## About Navattic 

With interactive demos becoming a very popular category over the last 12 months, there have been quite a few products in the market that are putting in their efforts to grab the piece of the pie. One of the earlier companies that started to help in the creation of interactive demos is Navattic. Growing marketing teams choose Navattic for its effective product tour software. 

However, the lack of a free trial, slow implementation, and higher pricing might prompt a search for an alternative product. With a **pricing** that starts from $500/month on annual billing, it can become hard to take a decision even without having an option to try the product out in the first place. 

If you are on the lookout for a demo platform that can offer much more coupled with competitive pricing structures, do consider these Navattic alternatives listed below.

## Alternatives to Navattic 

### Fable

**Best suited for**: Marketing & sales teams of all sizes

Fable is a user-friendly demo platform designed for marketing and sales teams to effortlessly create stunning interactive demos in just 5 minutes.

**Demo capabilities**: Screenshot & HTML demos

**Pros**:

- AI demo co-pilot: Get your demo up and running in under 5 minutes. Generate demo content, switch to various languages, create branding-specific themes, and instantly organize modules—all with simple, intuitive prompts.

- Start for free: Fable offers a free plan that lets you create your first interactive demo in just 5 minutes.

### Storylane

**Best suited for**: Sales & Marketing teams in growing SaaS companies

<img alt="Navattic alternative - Storylane" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/9b109d1f-c593-4b69-add6-f9baacabd2c5"></img>

Storylane is a versatile platform that can be used by sales as well as marketing teams of all sizes to create quick interactive demos.

**Demo capabilities**: Screenshot & HTML demos

**Pros**:

- Offers both screenshot & HTML demos: Teams can choose to try their screenshot demos or opt for their HTML capture option depending on their use cases.

- Fast implementation: With Storylane, you can get started quickly without involving any developers from your team.

- Integrates with sales and marketing tools: Storylane integrates with the likes of HubSpot, Segment, Google Analytics, among others to make it easy for sales & marketing teams to push data to their preferred tools.

**Cons**:

- Minimum of 5 users: Their HTML capture demos require you to pay for a minimum of 5 licenses even if you have only 1 user that intends to use the product.

- Storylane does not segment your demo viewers by device.

- Does not offer unlimited seats.

**Pricing**: Paid plans start from $50/user/month.

### Tourial

**Best suited for**: Marketing teams in growing SaaS companies

<img alt="Navattic alternative - Tourial" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/7764a5b8-d55d-4bf1-b7e7-f46f57fdacf1"></img>

Similar to Navattic, Tourial is a tool used by marketing teams to create engaging product tours. Marketers utilize Tourial’s demo center to present multiple product tours on a single landing page.

**Demo capabilities**: Screenshot & HTML demos

**Pros**:


- Create demo centers: Marketing teams can enable website visitors to choose their own journey based on use case, industry, and persona.

- Integrates with marketing tools: Tourial seamlessly integrates with tools like HubSpot and Marketo, allowing marketing teams to easily push data to their preferred platforms.

**Cons**:

- No fixed pricing: Tourial’s pricing is variable, depending on the features you need, your team size, and other factors.

- Limited analytics capabilities.

- Learning curve: Getting started with Tourial requires a learning curve, unlike some other platforms available today.

**Pricing**: Starts from $600 per month, paid annually.

### Demoboost

Best suited for: Pre-sales teams in mid-market companies

<img alt="Navattic alternative - Demoboost" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/48a64de9-3499-4776-a334-470758559c58"></img>

Demoboost is ideal for pre-sales teams to create demos and equip their AEs for demo calls.

**Demo capabilities**: HTML demos

**Pros**:

- Live demo features: Includes speaker notes, collaboration tools, and more.

- Multiple integrations: Seamlessly connects with HubSpot, Clearbit, Slack, and Google Analytics.

**Cons**:

- Learning curve: Requires some coding effort to customize demos.

- Performance issues: Some customer reviews mention unexpected lags, shutdowns, and resets.

**Pricing**: Starts from $800 per month, paid annually.

### Walnut

**Best suited for**: Sales teams in large companies

<img alt="Navattic alternative - Walnut" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/b97dbf32-27ab-428e-922d-dba6b08d5af4"></img>

Walnut is a demo platform with a clear focus on sales teams. It is designed for teams to share clickable demos post-calls as leave-behinds or use them during live demos.

**Demo capabilities**: Screenshot & HTML demos

**Pros**:

- Collaborate with prospects: Walnut enables prospects to comment and collaborate within the shared demos.

- Advanced features: These features help sales teams create personalized demos for all their prospects.

- Demo templates: Sales teams can create demo templates, which individual AEs can reuse to personalize demos for their prospects.

**Cons**:

- No free trial: Walnut does not offer a trial option before purchase.

- Unnecessary complexity: The platform can become complex for creating simple demos on a day-to-day basis.

- Steep learning curve: Getting started with Walnut requires a steep learning curve due to its complexity.

**Pricing**: Starts from $750 per month, paid annually.

### Reprise

**Best suited for**: Sales teams in enterprise companies

<img alt="Navattic alternative - Reprise" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/1594d69f-a896-4f52-a5f9-580978774c1b"></img>

Reprise is well-known for its Reveal product, which enterprise sales teams use to create demo environments instead of POCs.

**Demo capabilities**: Sandbox environments and HTML demos

**Pros**:

- Robust APIs: Their API infrastructure allows developers to manage complex use cases and develop advanced functionalities.

- Offline demos: Reprise supports offline demo capabilities, enabling teams to present sandbox demos at trade shows and events.

- Enterprise-grade security: With SOC2, GDPR, and CCPA compliance, Reprise provides top-tier security standards for demo platforms.

**Cons**:

- Significant engineering effort: Implementing Reprise requires substantial effort from your developers.

- Limited analytics capabilities.

- Learning curve: Using Reprise involves a learning curve compared to some other platforms.

**Pricing**: Starts at $2500 per month, billed annually.

- Pre-configured data sets: AEs can select from custom data sets designed for various industries.

**Cons**:

- Significant engineering effort: Implementing Testbox requires substantial effort from your developers as it operates as a live instance.

**Pricing**: Starts at $3000 per month, billed annually.

### Saleo

**Best suited for**: Sales teams in enterprise companies

<img alt="Navattic alternative - Saleo" src="https://documentden-deployments.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/public/40898ba2-9960-43c5-bddd-768542540731"></img>

Saleo is a sales demo platform designed to help teams create tailored demos for sales calls by overlaying customizations on top of your product.

**Demo capabilities**: Live demos

**Pros**:

- Customizable overlays: Allows you to create personalized demos by overlaying customizations on your product before each sales call.

- Easy customization: Modify your product using Saleo’s Chrome extension without the need for cloning.

**Cons**:

- Limited to sales teams: Not designed for marketing or customer success teams.

- Dependence on Saleo's team: Data modifications cannot be done without assistance from Saleo’s team, which can be a drawback for sales teams needing to frequently update demo data.

- High maintenance: Significant effort is required for updates or releases.

**Pricing**: Starts at $3300 per month, billed annually.

**Cons**:
